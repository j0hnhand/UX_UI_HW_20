Responsive Website Redesign

Gangstas to Growers

Gangstas to Growers provides employment, educational, and entrepreneurial opportunities in agriculture for at-risk and formerly incarcerated youth in west Atlanta. Their stakeholders wanted to increase donations and sales of their hot sauce while maintaining the website’s current design aesthetics. I worked as a UX/UI researcher and designer with a team to discover user pain points and provide a responsive website redesign helping stakeholders achieve their goals while satisfying user needs.

Research

Founder, Abiodun Henderson, wanted to create a way for people to donate to the cause, as well as increase sales for their only current product, Sweet Sol Hot Sauce that is made up of foods that members help grow, produce, bottle and sell as part of their co-operative. She also wanted to integrate the program’s strong social media presence and maintain the current website’s design aesthetic and branding.

We conducted surveys, stakeholder interviews, and usability tests to better understanding business needs and users’ pain points with the current website. This also helped us understand what might help Latonya decide to donate or make purchases.

Because the current site is limited in utility, we chose to map out our proposed user journey based on insights from our surveys, interviews, testing, user persona, feature selection, and sitemap.

Rather than using a template, we chose to create a custom user journey map reflecting the growth and vibrancy of our organization’s impacts as well as their brand colors.

Problem Statement

The Gangstas to Growers website is intended to be a portal allowing community-invested donors to feel connected to the local cause and make contributions. We have observed through surveys and interviews that users like Latonya Johnson want to see more information about the organization, transparency and benefits of the program. These shortcomings are causing a lack of donations and purchases, as well as a lack of understanding about the company.

How might we redesign the website in order to enhance available information, share successes and highlights, clearly define goals, connect with other local black-owned businesses, and help G2G achieve success in their mission based on a $500 increase in monthly donations, $500 increase in monthly sales, and a net promoter score over 7?

Iteration

We created paper prototypes based on the new site map. We chose to make two main pages so that the homepage could tell the story with general a general overview and clear calls to actions, while the about page could give more in-depth information.

Low-fidelity wireframe prototypes were then created to block out the respective sections and consider their placement. We decided to make the footer fixed while scrolling in order to keep social links and newsletter signup affordances prominent, but not as annoying or intrusive as a pop-up shadow box.

The mid-fidelity prototype was designed with a color palette inspired by the current brand colors and included assets from the current website. New copy was written based on the data gathered from stakeholder interviews.

Final Prototype

Our final iteration of the prototype included: updated global navigation as a result of our A/B testing, we changed out some of the images to better match the brand aesthetic and enhance accessibility, adjusted the size of the footer based on feedback from our user tests, and we updated the animated interactions for success stories and measured growth to reduce cognitive load
